Final Major Project - BA

My Final Major Project, which I created during my Batchelor Degree in Graphic Design, visually communicates The Beauty Myth, by Naomi Wolfe, and themes which relate to the text. The book sits underneath a cover, which represents the glass ceiling. Eteched onto the cover is a board room table, which is outlined by quotes from Aristotle and his peers, the chairs have been created with typography which uses FTSE 100 figures and their earnings.

I experimented with physical uses of paper within the book. The previous photographs display a tunnel made from many layered pages, with each layer representing a statistic, the figure of the statistic designated the size of the cut out. After many pages, you eventually reach a very small, but legible quote on gender inequality within society and how although women make up fifty per cent of the population, they make up two thirds of all working hours, receive less then one tenth of world income and own less than one percent of world property. The physical process represents the quote's meaning.

I embraced this project as an opportunity to visually communicate a text using a range of my skills, not only incorporating techniques with the physical use of paper but also utilising my photography skills. This photograph was placed at the beginning of the book, it may seem counter-intuitive but it represents that this information has consciously been removed from mainstream knowledge, and for those who benefit from these theories want this knowledge to remain hidden.

Throughout the book are die cuts which bring interest to the book, as they alter the companion pages as they hide and reveal information, while also being relevant to the pages it sits within.

The aim was to make the book tactile, to create intrigue and encourage interaction with the audience. Displayed is a spread where I incorporated different weights of paper, which is the play on the quotes in use, such as beauty as a currency system.
